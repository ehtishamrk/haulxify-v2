The NEMT Back Office Problem

Why NEMT billing is a full-time job — at minimum

NEMT operates at the intersection of healthcare, transportation, and government contracting. Each sector brings its own compliance demands. All three land on your desk.

50 State Medicaid Systems

Every state administers its own Medicaid program with unique billing codes, reimbursement rates, prior authorization requirements, and documentation standards. What works in Texas fails in Colorado. Navigating this alone is nearly impossible.

50 different state billing rules

Claim Denials Kill Cash Flow

A single missing field on a CMS-1500 form, an incorrect HCPCS code, or an expired eligibility verification can deny your entire claim. Denied claims require resubmission — adding weeks to your payment cycle and months of unrecovered revenue.

Denials are the #1 revenue killer in NEMT

HIPAA Documentation is Non-Negotiable

Every trip requires HIPAA-compliant handling of patient data — from scheduling through billing. Training, access controls, breach protocols, and documentation must all be maintained. One audit can expose your entire operation.

HIPAA training required for all staff

Driver Credentialing is Intensive

Every NEMT driver needs background checks, drug testing, first aid & CPR certification, HIPAA training, ADA compliance training, and vehicle inspection certification. Maintaining these credentials requires constant monitoring and renewal tracking.

10+ credentials per driver to maintain

Eligibility Verification Before Every Trip

You must verify a patient's Medicaid eligibility and transportation benefit before dispatching — every single trip. No verification, no payment. But verifying 50–200 trips per day manually takes hours of staff time that adds zero operational value.

Required before every single trip dispatch

Fraud Audit Risk Is Always Present

CMS actively investigates NEMT providers for billing fraud, waste, and abuse. Providers must maintain GPS-verified trip records, digital signatures, timestamped documentation, and audit-ready reports — or risk repayment demands and debarment.

Audits can recover years of past payments

Common Questions

NEMT back office FAQ

Is your team trained specifically on NEMT Medicaid billing?
Yes. Our NEMT team is specifically trained on CMS-1500 claim preparation, HCPCS coding (including codes like S0215, A0425), broker portal workflows for Modivcare and MTM, and HIPAA documentation standards. They don't learn on your account — they arrive ready.
Do you handle private pay billing as well as Medicaid?
Yes. We handle private pay invoicing separately from Medicaid billing. Private pay is simpler and faster — we generate invoices, track payments, and follow up on outstanding balances regardless of payer type.
How do you ensure HIPAA compliance from Pakistan?
All team members handling PHI sign Business Associate Agreements, undergo HIPAA training, work on secured devices, and access data only through encrypted channels with role-based access controls. Our processes meet the requirements for offshore BAA relationships under HIPAA.
Can you work with multiple Medicaid brokers simultaneously?
Absolutely. Many NEMT providers work with Modivcare, MTM, LogistiCare, and state brokers simultaneously. We document the specific workflow and requirements for each broker you work with and operate within each portal correctly.
What happens if a claim is denied after you prepare it?
We review the denial reason, correct the issue, and prepare the corrected claim for resubmission within 24–48 hours of the denial notice. We also log denial patterns to identify systemic issues and prevent repeat errors.
