The After-Hours Problem

What's actually happening when your phones go dark at 5pm

Transport doesn't run 9–5. Your competitors know that. The ones answering phones at midnight are taking your business.

Missed Calls Go Directly to Competitors

When a customer calls after hours and gets voicemail, they don't leave a message and wait. They call the next company on the list. That next company answers — and gets the job. Your missed call didn't just lose you one trip; it handed a customer relationship to your competition.

First to answer wins the job. Every time.

In-House Night Dispatch Costs $4–6K/Month

A dedicated overnight dispatcher — hired, trained, benefited, and insured — costs $48,000–$72,000 per year. For most small transport operators, that's not a dispatcher; that's the profit margin on their entire fleet for the year. The cost simply doesn't pencil out.

$48–72K/year for in-house overnight coverage

Driver Emergencies Don't Respect Business Hours

Breakdowns, accidents, missed deliveries, route problems — drivers call when things go wrong, not when your office is open. Without after-hours coverage, your drivers are stranded on the phone with no support, and your customers are waiting with no updates.

Driver emergencies happen most often at night

Owner On-Call Burnout Is Real

When there's no overnight dispatcher, the owner carries the phone. Sleeping with one eye open — answering calls at 2am, dealing with driver problems at 4am — isn't a business strategy. It's a path to burnout that eventually makes every decision worse.

Owner on-call is not sustainable past a few vehicles

Booking Gaps From Overnight Delay

Requests that come in overnight and aren't responded to until 8am often find a different provider by morning. The customer who emailed at 11pm or submitted an online form at midnight expects a response — and when your competitor is monitoring and responding, you're not even in the conversation.

Overnight booking requests lost by morning every day

Weekends and Holidays Are Black Holes

Transport volume doesn't slow on weekends or holidays — it often spikes. Memorial Day moves. Holiday freight. Weekend tows. If your operation goes dark on Saturday afternoon and doesn't return until Monday morning, you're leaving two full revenue days uncovered every week.

Weekends uncovered = 28% of all potential weekly revenue lost

Common Questions

After-hours dispatch FAQ

Will callers know they're not reaching your in-house team?
No. We answer using your business name, your greeting, and your protocols. To callers, it's indistinguishable from an in-house dispatcher. We represent your brand — not ours.
How are calls forwarded to your team?
You set a time-based forward on your main business number — so calls route to us automatically after hours. We can also set up a dedicated after-hours number if you prefer to keep lines separate. Setup takes minutes and requires no hardware.
Can you handle after-hours for multiple phone lines or departments?
Yes. We can handle multiple incoming lines with different protocols — dispatch calls, customer service calls, driver support calls — each handled according to the appropriate script and workflow for that line type.
What time zone does your team work in?
Our team operates from Pakistan. This means Pakistan's business hours naturally overlap with US overnight and early-morning hours — making us a natural fit for after-hours US coverage. Your 10pm call is our 8am. Your 4am emergency is our noon.
