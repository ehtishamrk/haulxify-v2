Sprinter & Van Reality

Why most cargo van operators never scale past two vehicles

The van freight market looks accessible from the outside. Inside, it's a margin war. Here's what keeps operators stuck.

Brokers Lowball Van Freight Rates

Because van operators are abundant and the barrier to entry is low, freight brokers have leverage. Without professional negotiation support and carrier authority credentials, van operators routinely accept rates that barely cover fuel — let alone profit.

Van rates compressed by market oversupply

Deadhead Miles Bleed Margin

Every mile driven without a load is pure cost. Van operators without consistent lane coverage and backhaul coordination can spend 30–40% of their total miles deadheading — paying fuel costs to move nothing and make nothing.

30–40% deadhead rates destroy profitability

Feast-or-Famine Load Availability

Van freight is volatile. A good week on the load board is followed by two slow ones. Without a diversified broker network, consistent shipper relationships, and proactive load searching — cash flow is unpredictable and planning is impossible.

Volatile load availability without broker network support

Carrier Setup and Compliance Takes Time

Getting properly set up with brokers — carrier packets, insurance certificates, W-9, MC authority verification — takes time and must be maintained. Most van operators are so busy driving that their carrier setup falls behind, limiting broker relationships.

Hours to set up and maintain each broker relationship

Slow Invoicing = Slow Cash

Submitting invoices manually after every load, following up on payments, and managing accounts receivable takes hours every week. When you're driving and running admin simultaneously, invoicing falls behind — and so does your cash flow.

Delayed invoicing directly delays cash flow

Hiring Drivers Requires Infrastructure You Don't Have

Growing beyond one van means hiring drivers. But most van operators have no hiring process, no onboarding documents, and no HR infrastructure. Every growth attempt stalls because adding headcount is too complicated to execute alone.

No hiring infrastructure = permanent solo operation

Common Questions

Sprinter & cargo van FAQ

Do you work with straight truck and sprinter operators without MC authority?
Yes. We work with operators at all authority levels — from those with full MC authority to those running under another carrier's authority. If you need help setting up your own MC authority, we can guide you through that process as well.
Can you help find loads for specific freight types like medical supplies or pharmaceuticals?
Yes. We work to find loads that match your vehicle type, preferred freight categories, and lane preferences. Specialized freight — medical, high-value, time-critical — often pays better and we target those categories for operators who are properly set up for them.
What load boards do you actively monitor for van freight?
We primarily use DAT and Truckstop for van freight, supplemented by direct broker channels on lanes where you have existing relationships. We also maintain contact with brokers who specialize in sprinter and expedite freight for higher-rate opportunities.
