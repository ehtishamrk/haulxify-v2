The Towing Business Reality

What's actually killing tow truck profit margins

There are 39,000+ towing businesses in the US. Almost none of them can afford a full administrative team. Here's what that costs.

24/7 Operations With No Night Staff

Towing doesn't stop at 5pm. Accidents happen at midnight. Stranded motorists call at 3am. But hiring a full-time night dispatcher costs $4,000–$6,000 per month — an expense most small tow operators simply cannot justify.

$4–6K/month for in-house night dispatch

Motor Club Rates Are Below Cost

AAA, Agero, Allstate Motor Club — they all pay below-market rates and then slow-walk payment. Many operators can't afford to turn down the volume, but the motor club calls they take often cost more to service than they pay out.

Motor club rates often below true cost per call

Insurance Billing Disputes Never End

Getting paid by third-party insurance companies requires documentation, follow-up calls, resubmissions, and sometimes legal threat letters. Each dispute takes hours — and unpaid invoices stack up fast when you're running multiple trucks.

Insurance disputes average weeks to resolve

Driver Retention Is a Constant Fight

Tow truck drivers work in high-stress, physically demanding conditions. Turnover is endemic in the industry. Every departure means rehiring costs, training time, and temporary service gaps — often at the worst possible moments.

High turnover across the entire industry

Missing Calls Equals Lost Revenue

Every call that goes unanswered is a customer who calls your competitor. In towing, first response wins the job. If your dispatcher is handling another call, eating dinner, or asleep — that revenue goes somewhere else permanently.

1 missed call = 1 job lost, forever

Bookkeeping Falls Behind

Between dispatching, driving, and chasing payments, tow operators rarely have time for clean books. Tax season becomes a crisis. Cash flow is managed by gut feel. Profit per call is unknown until the accountant delivers bad news.

Most tow operators can't tell you net per call

Common Questions

Tow truck back office FAQ

Can you dispatch for motor clubs directly?
Yes. We handle motor club job acceptance, tracking, and documentation across major motor clubs including AAA, Agero, Allstate, and others you're contracted with. We log all jobs in your system and track mileage and extras for accurate billing.
How does after-hours dispatch work?
Your calls forward to our team. We answer professionally under your business name, assess the call, and dispatch your available driver. We text or call drivers directly and log every job. You see the full call log when you start your day.
Can you help with insurance claim disputes?
We handle the documentation and follow-up process for insurance billing. We prepare invoices with all required documentation, submit claims, follow up on outstanding amounts, and escalate disputes. We do not provide legal representation but handle all the administrative work that drives most billing fights.
What's the minimum number of trucks to work with you?
No minimum. We work with single-truck operators all the way up to regional fleets. In fact, solo operators benefit the most — because you have zero back office capacity and every hour you spend on dispatch or billing is an hour you're not on the hook.
