Pricing Questions

Common questions about how we bill

How are the 176 monthly hours calculated?
We bill 8 standard hours per weekday, Monday through Friday. A typical month has 22 working days × 8 hours = 176 hours. Weekends and major US public holidays are included in your plan at no extra charge — your team is available, but you're not billed for those hours.
How does weekly billing work?
We work Monday through Friday. Your invoice is issued every Friday for that week's work (40 hours). Payment is due by the following Monday. If payment is not received by Monday morning, services are paused until the balance is cleared. This policy keeps things fair for the entire operation — no exceptions.
Why do monthly and quarterly plans require advance payment?
Pre-payment is what allows us to offer the 15% and 20% discounts. When you commit upfront, we commit dedicated team slots upfront. We staff your account from day one — advance payment ensures we can do that without risk on either side. Think of it as a retainer, not a deposit.
What does the load board fee cover?
Services like trucking dispatch, freight brokerage, hotshot, and car hauling require access to a load board (DAT, Truckstop.io, Central Dispatch, etc.) to source and post loads. If you already have a subscription, you simply share access with your Haulxify team — no extra charge. If you don't, we add $100/month or $25/week to your bill to cover it.
What if my fleet grows or shrinks?
Notify us and we adjust. Weekly clients: changes take effect the following week. Monthly and quarterly clients: we adjust at the next billing cycle and credit or invoice the difference. Seasonal operators scale up and down all the time — we handle it without penalties.
Are there setup fees or long-term contracts?
No setup fees. No long-term contracts. Weekly clients can stop with one week's notice. Monthly and quarterly plans are pre-paid for that period — when the period ends, you renew or walk away. We earn your continued business every cycle, not through contractual lock-in.
