Pricing FAQ

Common pricing questions

How does the weekly billing cycle work?
We run Monday to Friday. Your invoice is sent on Friday for that week's work, and payment is due by end of Monday. If payment isn't received, service is paused until cleared. No late fees — just timely payment keeps everything running smoothly.
What does "176 hours per month" mean?
Monthly billing is based on 8 working hours per day × 22 average working days = 176 hours. Weekends are not billed. Major public holidays are also excluded, making monthly plans excellent value for standard operations.
How does pricing work for 2 or 3 units?
The first unit (vehicle, line, or agent) is $5.00/hr. The 2nd and 3rd units are $3.50/hr each. The pricing calculator on this page shows both the per-unit rate and the combined total hourly rate so there is no ambiguity. For 4 or more units, we offer custom bulk pricing with further discounts.
Do monthly and quarterly plans require upfront payment?
Yes — both monthly and quarterly plans require full payment in advance. This is how we can offer the 15% and 20% discounts. Your dedicated team is allocated from day one, and the advance payment ensures continuity for both sides.
What is the load board requirement?
For dispatch-dependent services (Trucking, Car Hauler), clients should provide access to their DAT, Truckstop, or similar load board. If you prefer not to share access, we source loads through our own accounts for $100/month or $25/week.
Are there contracts or cancellation fees?
No contracts, no setup fees, no cancellation penalties. Weekly plans can stop at any time. Monthly and quarterly plans are prepaid for their term, but there is no obligation beyond that period. We earn continued business by delivering results, not by locking you in.
