Animal Hauling Compliance Reality

What makes animal transport back office uniquely complex

You're not just hauling freight. You're hauling living cargo under federal, state, and often species-specific regulations that most general logistics companies have never heard of.

USDA Certificate of Veterinary Inspection

A CVI (health certificate) signed by an accredited veterinarian is required for most interstate livestock and equine transport. These certificates have strict validity windows, species-specific requirements, and must match exactly what's on the truck at the border crossing.

CVI errors at state borders = turned-around loads

State Entry Requirements Vary Wildly

Every state has its own entry requirements for livestock, equine, and exotic animals. Breed certifications, brand inspections, health certificates, and import permits all vary by state and species. A load that's compliant to enter Texas may need completely different documentation to pass through Oklahoma.

50 states, 50 different entry requirement stacks

Driver Credential Requirements Are Specialized

Drivers hauling livestock or horses need more than a CDL. Animal handling certifications, species-specific training, welfare compliance knowledge, and sometimes USDA-registered status are all requirements that must be documented, tracked, and renewed.

Multiple specialized credentials per driver to maintain

Transport Time Restrictions for Live Animals

DOT and USDA regulations limit how long animals can be in transit without rest, feed, and water. Record-keeping for these stops is not optional — it's federal documentation. Non-compliance results in fines, license suspension, and animal welfare investigations.

Federal documentation required for every transit stop

Seasonal Volume Creates Hiring Chaos

Livestock markets, breeding seasons, fair circuits, and auction calendars create predictable but intense volume spikes. When demand spikes, you need more drivers — vetted, credentialed, and ready — faster than any generic hiring process can deliver.

Seasonal peaks require fast credentialed driver hiring

Standard Back Office Tools Don't Understand Your Industry

Generic TMS and dispatch software isn't built for CVI tracking, rest stop documentation, or species-specific billing. Most animal haulers are cobbling together spreadsheets, paper logs, and reminder apps to manage compliance that deserves a dedicated system.

No industry-built tools = manual compliance management

Common Questions

Animal hauler back office FAQ

Do you have experience with equine transport specifically?
Yes. Equine transport is one of the most documentation-intensive animal hauling categories and we're familiar with CVI requirements, brand inspection certificates, Coggins tests, and interstate equine entry requirements for all major horse-transit states.
Can you track CVI expiration windows across multiple animals on one load?
Yes. When a load carries multiple animals with separate health certificates — which is common in equine and small livestock transport — we track each certificate individually and confirm all are valid for the full transit window before dispatch authorization.
What livestock species do you have documentation experience with?
We have documentation experience across equine, bovine, swine, poultry, sheep, and goats — covering the federal and state requirements for each. For more exotic species or USDA-permitted animals, we conduct specific research for each transit to ensure compliance before dispatch.
