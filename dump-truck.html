The Paper Ticket Problem

Why dump truck operators leave money on the table every week

The aggregate and construction hauling industry is one of the last holdouts on paper-based operations. That choice isn't free — it costs revenue, time, and margin on every job.

Lost Tickets = Unbilled Loads

Paper tickets end up under truck seats, soaked in rain, or simply lost during handoffs. Every missing ticket is a load you delivered but cannot bill. On a 50-load day across 8 drivers, even a 5% loss rate means money that never comes back.

Lost tickets = direct revenue loss with no recovery

Counting Tickets Takes Hours Every Day

Drivers and dispatchers spending hours in fast food parking lots counting and reconciling paper tickets is an industry joke — except nobody's laughing. That time has a real cost in wages, and it still produces errors that delay invoicing by days.

Hours lost daily to manual ticket reconciliation

Subcontractor Ticket Fraud

Without verification systems, some drivers fabricate tickets for loads that never happened. In large fleets with multiple subcontractors running simultaneously, detecting fraud manually is nearly impossible — and it adds up to significant overpayments.

Ticket fraud is common and nearly undetectable manually

Billing Per Ton vs Per Load vs Per Hour

Construction clients bill differently. Some want tonnage invoices from scale tickets. Others want per-load counts. Others pay hourly. Managing these variations manually across multiple active jobs with different billing structures guarantees errors and disputes.

Multiple billing structures per job site

Invoicing Gets Done Days After Delivery

By the time paper tickets are collected, reconciled, entered into a spreadsheet, and invoiced, it's often 3–7 days after the work was completed. Construction clients have their own payment terms — and late invoicing means later payment, every time.

3–7 day invoicing delay is standard on paper systems

Seasonal Feast-or-Famine Hiring

Construction slows in winter and explodes in spring. Dump truck operators scramble to hire and onboard drivers fast every season — then manage layoffs when work dries up. Without an efficient hiring process, you're always a step behind the construction calendar.

Seasonal swings require constant rapid hiring

Common Questions

Dump truck back office FAQ

We're still on paper tickets. Can you work with that?
Yes, we can work with photo uploads of paper tickets as a starting point. However, our strong recommendation is to transition to digital ticketing — and we can help you implement a simple driver app solution that your team can learn in under an hour. The revenue recovery from eliminating lost tickets typically pays for everything within the first month.
Can you handle billing if we have different rates for different clients?
Absolutely. We set up a rate table for each client relationship — per ton, per load, per hour, or any combination — and invoice automatically using the correct rate for each job. No manual calculation. No rate confusion. No billing disputes from using the wrong rate.
How do you handle subcontractor pay when their ticket counts don't match ours?
We reconcile subcontractor-submitted ticket counts against your dispatch records and scale house data. Discrepancies are flagged and documented before pay statements are issued — so every dispute has a clear paper trail and you're not paying for loads that didn't happen.
Can you ramp up hiring support quickly for construction season?
Yes. Give us 2–4 weeks before your peak season ramp and we'll have your job listings posted, incoming applications screened, CDLs verified, and qualified candidates ready for final interviews with you. We can process multiple candidates simultaneously so you're never short-staffed when spring projects start.
